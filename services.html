Specialties & Interests

Trauma

Trauma therapy is designed to help individuals process and heal from the effects of traumatic experiences. It allows individuals to manage symptoms like flashbacks, triggers, and intense emotions by developing coping mechanisms and building resilience in a safe and supportive environment. The ultimate aim is to reduce the impact of trauma on daily life.

Life Changes

Significant events in our lives, such as graduation, marriage, parenthood, retirement, and loss, can have a profound impact.

These can include:

  • Major Life Milestones: Significant events like graduation, marriage, parenthood, and retirement.
  • Developmental Stages: The natural progression through different life phases with associated changes.
  • Turning Points: Moments that significantly alter the course of someone's life.
  • Life Phases: Distinct periods within a person's life with unique characteristics.
  • Transformative Periods: Times of significant personal growth and change.

Dissociative Identity Disorder

A disorder characterized by the presence of two or more distinct personality states. Dissociative identity disorder, previously called multiple personality disorder, is usually a reaction to trauma as a way to help a person avoid bad memories.


Dissociative identity disorder is characterized by the presence of two or more distinct personality identities. Each may have a unique name, personal history, and characteristics. Treatment is talk therapy.
